## Tuesdays — won't you pause for a moment?

Daily life and work keep our minds racing. Before we know it, the day ends without our minds ever truly resting.

This meditation circle is a place to ease that tension and spend quiet time once a week. It's structured so you can join directly after work, in a way that's easy to keep up with.

## Who it's for

- Your mind never stops racing
- You want to shift gears emotionally
- You want time to truly relax

The venue is a quiet temple in Utsunomiya, Tochigi. An experienced Thai monk guides the session in gentle Japanese. Even if it's your first time meditating, you can start with the basics — posture and breath — at your own pace.

> Note: This is not a religious or recruiting event.

This is an 8-session course, but single-session attendance is welcome too. Through a different theme each time — a short talk and meditation — you'll gradually learn ways to settle the mind and body in everyday life.

## About the course

A meditation course to step away from the busyness of work and daily life and bring the mind and body back into balance.

It's designed so beginners can start with ease — beginning with the basics of posture and breath, then progressing step by step through concentration, stability, and applying it to daily life. Across all 8 sessions, the goal is to make meditation not something "special," but a useful everyday habit. Each session pairs a themed talk with practice, and single-session attendance is welcome.

## The 8-Session Program — Three Steps

### 1. Settle (Sessions 1–2) — Build the foundation

- **Session 1** ｜ Posture & breath: the foundation of meditation
- **Session 2** ｜ Concentration with words (mantra meditation)

Loosen body and mind, and create a state where you can practice with ease.

### 2. Use (Sessions 3–6) — Cultivate the mind and concentration

- **Session 3** ｜ Working with the mind without being swept away by thoughts
- **Session 4** ｜ Focus that flows (flow state)
- **Session 5** ｜ The sense of returning to your center
- **Session 6** ｜ What happens in the mind and brain during meditation

Deepen the core sensations and practices of meditation.

### 3. Integrate (Sessions 7–8) — Connect it to daily life

- **Session 7** ｜ A peaceful mind and relationships
- **Session 8** ｜ A meditation habit for daily life

Bring meditation into a form you can use naturally, day to day.

## What a session looks like

1. Doors open / reception
2. Relaxation (herbal tea) — sip warm tea and gently let the day's tension unwind
3. Dhamma talk — practical, easy-to-follow "mind care" for daily life
4. Meditation — guided by the monk, at a comfortable pace
5. Closing

After the session there's an optional time for questions and casual conversation — ask the monk anything, share impressions with other participants, and take it slow.
