## Organization

Sabai Mind NPO (特定非営利活動法人 Sabai Mind)

In Thai, **"Sabai"** means "comfortable, at ease." We blend traditional Thai Buddhist wisdom with the Japanese aesthetic of stillness, creating "places of mental rest" for modern society.

## Message from the Chairman

Chairman **Iso Minoru (磯 實)**

> In Japan, approximately 1 in 15 people will experience depression in their lifetime. In an era when mental illness is no longer a distant concern, we focus on **prevention** and **everyday accessibility**.
>
> Mindfulness is highly regarded in Western medicine and business as a method for managing depression and pain. We want to bring that wisdom to Japan in a form anyone can adopt — beyond the bounds of religion. That is the wish of Sabai Mind.

## Mission

To bring Thai mindfulness to Japan and build a society where everyone can recover peace of mind in daily life.

## Two Pillars of Our Work

### 1. Promoting Thai Mindfulness

- In-person and online meditation practice sessions
- Seminars at schools, companies, communities, and children's homes
- Outreach via social media and printed materials
- Dispatching experienced Thai monks as meditation instructors

### 2. Community Exchange Through Thai Culture

- Songkran Festival (the traditional Thai New Year and water festival)
- Sky Lantern Festival (releasing wishes into the night sky)
- Mindfulness workshops rooted in the local community

## Meditation Instructor Dispatch

We send Thai monks who have trained for years in meditation to schools, companies, hospitals, and community events anywhere in Japan. Available in Japanese, English, Chinese, and Thai. **We never proselytize.**

The cost is travel expenses plus a voluntary offering. In Kantō, Kōshin'etsu, Chūbu, and Kinki regions we have resident monks, so transportation costs can be kept low.
